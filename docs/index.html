How Volux Works

Simple setup, automatic from then on

1

Install the Extension

Add Volux to Chrome or Firefox with one click. No account needed, no complex setup. The extension icon appears in your browser toolbar immediately.

2

Adjust Volume on Any Website

Click the Volux icon and use the slider to set your preferred volume for that website. YouTube too loud? Slide it down to 30%. Podcast site too quiet? Boost it up.

3

Volux Remembers Forever

That's it. Volux automatically saves your preference and applies it every time you visit that site. Refresh the page, close the browser, come back next week - your volume settings stay exactly as you set them.

Pro tip: All tabs from the same website share the volume setting. Set YouTube once, and all your YouTube tabs (current and future) will use that volume level automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Volux

Why does my browser volume reset every time I refresh?

Browsers don't save volume settings per website by default. Every time you refresh or reopen a tab, the volume resets to 100%. Volux solves this by automatically remembering your preferred volume for each website, so you never have to adjust it again.

How do I set different volume for YouTube and Spotify?

With Volux, each website has its own independent volume slider. Simply set YouTube to 30%, Spotify to 80%, or any level you prefer. Volux saves these settings automatically and applies them every time you visit those sites.

Why is YouTube always too loud compared to other sites?

Different websites have different audio levels. YouTube videos are often mastered louder than other content. Volux lets you set a permanent lower volume for YouTube (or any loud website) so you never get blasted with sudden loud audio again.

Does Volux remember my settings after I close the browser?

Yes! Your volume settings are saved permanently. Close Chrome, restart your computer, come back days later - Volux remembers exactly how you set the volume for each website. No more manual adjustments.

Can I control volume for Zoom, Google Meet, or Microsoft Teams?

Absolutely. Volux works with any website that plays audio, including video conferencing apps like Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams. Keep your meeting audio at a comfortable level while other tabs play at different volumes.

Is Volux safe? What permissions does it need?

Volux is open source and privacy-friendly. It only requests permissions necessary to control audio on web pages. We don't collect any personal data, browsing history, or analytics. You can review our code on GitHub.

What's the difference between Free and Pro?

The Free version lets you control volume for 2 websites - perfect for trying it out. Pro ($2 one-time) unlocks unlimited websites, auto-detection of media sites, and the ability to scan all open tabs for audio. It's a lifetime purchase, no subscription.

Does Volux work with multiple tabs of the same website?

Yes! All tabs from the same website share the same volume setting. If you set YouTube to 40%, all your YouTube tabs will be at 40%. This makes managing multiple tabs simple and consistent.
