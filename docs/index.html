Pro · Tab Override

One site. Three tabs. Three volumes.

Most extensions give you one volume per website. Volux Pro lets every tab of any site — YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, Spotify, Twitch — hold its own level independently, without touching the site default.

  • Expand Tap the N TABS badge on any domain to reveal every open tab with a dedicated slider.
  • Override Drag any tab’s slider to give it its own volume. The site default still covers anything you haven’t customized.
  • Dispose Close the tab or navigate away — the override clears automatically. Your site default stays clean.

06 · Questions

Frequently asked.

Tap any question to expand. Shift-click to open in a new reading frame.

Why does my browser volume reset every time I refresh?
Browsers don’t save volume settings per website by default. Every refresh or reopened tab resets to 100%. Volux solves this by automatically remembering your preferred volume for each website, so you never have to adjust it again.
How do I set different volumes for YouTube and Spotify?
Each website gets its own independent volume slider. Set YouTube to 30%, Spotify to 80%, or any level you prefer. Volux saves these settings automatically and applies them every time you visit those sites.
Why is YouTube always too loud compared to other sites?
Different websites master audio at different levels. YouTube videos often ship louder than other content. Volux lets you pin a permanent lower volume for YouTube (or any loud website) so you never get blasted again.
Does Volux remember my settings after I close the browser?
Yes. Settings are saved in your browser’s local storage. Close Chrome, restart your computer, return days later — Volux applies exactly the levels you set for each website.
Can I control volume for Zoom, Google Meet, or Microsoft Teams?
Yes. Volux works with any website that plays audio, including video conferencing apps. Keep meeting audio at a comfortable level while other tabs play at different volumes.
Is Volux safe? What permissions does it need?
Volux is open source and privacy-friendly. It only requests permissions necessary to control audio on web pages. No personal data, no browsing history, no analytics. Code is reviewable on GitHub.
What’s the difference between Free and Pro?
Free lets you control volume for 2 websites — perfect to try. Pro ($2 one-time) unlocks unlimited websites, auto-detection of media sites, and the ability to scan all open tabs. Lifetime purchase, no subscription.
Does Volux work with multiple tabs of the same website?
Yes — two modes. By default, all tabs of the same site share one volume. With Pro, you can override any individual tab: three tabs on the same site can hold three different volumes simultaneously, and the site default still covers any tab you haven’t customized.
Can I set different volumes for multiple tabs of the same site (YouTube, TikTok, Facebook…)?
Yes, with Volux Pro. It works on any site with audio — YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, Spotify, Twitch, SoundCloud, news sites, anywhere. Click the “N tabs” badge on a domain card to expand a per-tab list, then drag the slider on any tab to give it its own volume. The override clears when you close that tab or navigate it to a different site, so your site-wide default stays clean.
